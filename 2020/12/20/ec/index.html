Labor Market

Yesterday in the economic lessons,I leaned about the knowledge of “Labor Market” which is closely related to our real life.

There are many people finding works,but the occupations are scarce. As a result,there are also a wide range of factors that may influence a person’s choice of occupations including wage factors,non-wage factors and other limiting factors.

Wage factors,of course,is the most important element. Generally speaking,the higher the salaries on offer,the more a person would want to do the job. Wage rate means the basic wage a worker receives per unit. I think if I need to find a job,I may think of it first. Due to the life quantity depends on incomes a lot. A high bonuses or commission may attract someone too,but not every job has extra bonuses.

Moreover,some other non-wage factors may be attached importance to by people. I will list a few the most principal reasons. First,some jobs can provide a high degree of job satisfaction,which would result in a higher degree of motivation. For example,most teachers have happiness during their whole teaching career. Not only the success of their students in study,but also receiving thanks from parents. That is the pride of all their lifetime. Second,the length of holidays makes a big difference. Although the government have made laws to set down a minimum length for full-time workers. In Western countries,some companies may provide more holidays for delivery mothers,newly married couples and so on.

From this lesson,I learned that everyone have their different choices for a job. All in all,the most suitable one is the best!
